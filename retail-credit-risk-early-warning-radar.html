How the early-warning logic works

Each indicator carries two calibrated thresholds — a green/amber boundary and an amber/red boundary — the same structure as a standard risk appetite statement. But a static threshold check only tells you about a problem once it has already arrived. So indicators still inside the green zone are also checked for a building trend: three consecutive months of worsening readings with a meaningful relative increase get flagged Watch — a lead indicator, ahead of any breach. That's the actual point of an early-warning framework over a plain RAG dashboard: time to act before escalation is forced.

Why these eight indicators

They're grouped the way a risk committee would expect to see them: credit performance (delinquency, charge-offs), behavioural and affordability signals (utilisation, minimum-payment reliance), concentration (geography, thin-file origination), and macro/external pressure (unemployment sensitivity, cost-of-living). Each card carries an owner and an escalation tier mapped to the three lines of defence.
